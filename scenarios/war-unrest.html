War AND Unrest

War and civil unrest are the scenarios people least want to think about and most need to prepare for. Unlike natural disasters, which are impersonal, conflict is driven by human decision-making: unpredictable, politically charged, and capable of escalating from manageable to catastrophic with very little warning.

For civilians, the experience of conflict is not combat. It is scarcity, uncertainty, and fear. It is supply chains that stop overnight. It is infrastructure that gets damaged and not repaired. It is the sudden realization that the institutions you relied on: police, hospitals, grocery stores, banks: are either overwhelmed, compromised, or gone. The survivors of the Bosnian War, the Syrian Civil War, and the Venezuelan collapse all describe the same thing: a slow disbelief followed by rapid adaptation.

The preparations for war and unrest overlap heavily with other scenarios but with two critical additions: security becomes the top priority, and mobility becomes essential. You may need to defend your home. You may need to leave it. The people who survive conflict are those who prepared for both possibilities and had the information to decide which one to choose.

Your Move
01 Secure your home
Home hardening and defense
Door reinforcement, window security film, motion-activated lighting, and personal defense tools. In civil unrest, your home is your first line of protection. Make it harder to breach than your neighbor's.
Security AND Defense
Cash and documents ready to move
If you need to leave quickly, cash and identity documents are what let you cross borders, buy passage, and start over. Have them organized, accessible, and in a grab-ready bag.
Wealth AND Documents
CBRN protection
Gas masks with NBC-rated filters, potassium iodide tablets, plastic sheeting for room sealing, and a decontamination kit. Modern conflict includes chemical, biological, radiological, and nuclear threats. Protection is available and affordable.
Security AND Defense
02 Sustain without supply chains
Food and water independence
Conflict disrupts supply chains instantly and indefinitely. A deep food stockpile and reliable water filtration mean you are not forced into dangerous situations to meet basic needs.
Food AND SupplyWater AND Hydration
Off-grid power
Power infrastructure is a target in conflict. Solar panels and a battery system keep essential devices running: lights, communication, medical equipment, refrigeration for medications.
Energy AND Power
03 Stay informed, stay mobile
Radio and communication
Information determines survival in conflict. Who controls what area, where is safe, which routes are open. A radio and two-way communication devices keep you connected when cell networks fail.
Communication AND Navigation
Repair and field tools
In prolonged conflict, nothing gets professionally repaired. Multi-tools, duct tape, cordage, and basic mechanical skills keep your shelter, transport, and equipment functional.

Essential reading
Cover
The Dark Secrets of SHTF Survival
Selco Begovic
Written by a civilian who survived the Bosnian War siege of his city for a year without running water, electricity, or reliable food supply. Begovic describes what actually happens when society collapses around you: the social dynamics, the barter economy, the security threats, and the psychological toll. The most honest first-person account of civilian conflict survival available.

Cover
Nuclear War: A Scenario
Annie Jacobsen
A minute-by-minute reconstruction of what would happen if a nuclear weapon were launched at the United States. Jacobsen interviewed dozens of military and government officials to build a detailed, terrifying, and factual account of escalation, impact, and aftermath. Not a preparedness manual, but essential context for the most extreme end of this scenario.

Cover
Left of Bang: How the Marine Corps' Combat Hunter Program Can Save Your Life
Patrick Van Horne and Jason A. Riley
Adapted from the Marine Corps' threat detection program for civilian use. Teaches how to read body language, identify threats before they materialize, and make faster decisions under pressure. The core skill is situational awareness: seeing danger before it arrives. Applicable to everything from urban unrest to travel in unstable regions.

Clint Emerson
Written by a retired Navy SEAL, this is a visual field guide to operational survival skills: escaping restraints, navigating without GPS, creating improvised tools, moving through hostile territory, and surviving captivity. Presented as illustrated step-by-step instructions. Compact, practical, and designed for real-world application.
