Security & Defense

Preparedness without security is just stockpiling for someone else. When normal systems of order are disrupted — whether for hours or months — the ability to protect your household, your supplies, and your people becomes the foundation everything else rests on. This section covers home hardening, surveillance, perimeter awareness, personal defense tools, and the mindset that ties it all together.

Most residential doors and windows can be defeated in seconds. Home hardening is about buying time — making your home difficult enough to breach that an intruder either gives up or gives you time to react. None of this requires a construction crew. Most upgrades are bolt-on, DIY-friendly, and cost less than a single insurance deductible. The philosophy is simple: deter, delay, defend — in that order.

Door Reinforcement
Weakest Point, Biggest Impact
The front door is breached in 90% of home invasions, and it usually takes one kick. The problem isn't the door itself — it's the strike plate. Standard strike plates are held by ¾ inch screws into soft pine trim. Replacing them with reinforced strike plates secured by 3 inch screws that reach the wall studs turns a one-kick entry into a many-kick ordeal. Door armor kits (like Door Armor MAX) reinforce the strike plate, hinge side, and door edge simultaneously. Pair with a heavy-duty deadbolt and you've hardened the most vulnerable entry point in your home for under $100.
3" screws into studs Biggest security ROI Price: $40–100
Window Security
Glass Is Not a Barrier
Standard window glass shatters with a single hit. Security window film (like 3M Safety & Security Film) holds shattered glass in place, turning a quick smash-and-entry into a sustained effort that creates noise and delay. It doesn't make glass unbreakable — it makes it resistant enough to buy time. Additional measures: window bars or grilles (controversial aesthetically, effective practically), window pin locks that prevent sliding windows from being forced, and thorny landscaping beneath ground-floor windows. For sliding glass doors: a security bar in the track is the simplest effective upgrade.
Security film holds glass Pin locks for sliders Thorny plants as deterrent
Door Barricades & Security Bars
Last Line at the Door
Door barricade devices mount at the base of the door and brace against the floor — distributing forced-entry impact across the entire floor rather than a few screws. Products like the Nightlock and Master Lock Door Security Bar can withstand several hundred pounds of force. They're particularly useful for apartments (where you can't modify the frame), hotel rooms, and safe-room doors. Some are portable enough for travel. Pair with door reinforcement for a layered approach — the reinforced frame resists the kick, and the barricade absorbs what gets through.
Floor-braced resistance Works in apartments Portable travel options

You can't defend against what you can't see coming. Surveillance and early warning systems give you the critical advantage of time — seconds to minutes of advance notice that someone is approaching your property. In normal times, cameras and smart doorbells deter porch pirates. When things deteriorate, the same equipment becomes your early warning network. The best systems work without Wi-Fi or grid power.

Security Cameras
Eyes When You Can't Watch
Outdoor security cameras provide 24/7 visual coverage of entry points and property lines. For preparedness, prioritize cameras with local storage (SD card or NVR) over cloud-only systems — when the internet goes down, cloud cameras become blind. Solar-powered models with battery backup operate indefinitely off-grid. Night vision (infrared) is non-negotiable for any useful security camera. Systems like Reolink and Eufy offer local storage, solar power options, and no monthly subscriptions. Position cameras to cover all entry points and the driveway approach.
Local storage essential Solar + battery for off-grid Night vision required
Driveway & Perimeter Alarms
Early Warning
Motion-activated alarms positioned at property entry points — driveways, gates, paths — that alert you inside the house when someone approaches. Wireless models (like the Guardline or Chamberlain CWA2000) use a sensor outdoors and a receiver indoors, with ranges up to ½ mile. Different chime tones for different sensors let you know which approach is active. Battery-powered sensors require no wiring. In a grid-down scenario, these passive systems continue working on batteries long after smart home systems have died. The low-tech solution that provides the highest-value early warning.
Battery powered Up to ½ mile range No Wi-Fi required
Motion-Sensor Lights
Simplest Deterrent
Bright lights that activate when motion is detected — the single most effective and cheapest security upgrade for any property. Criminals avoid lit areas because illumination removes their advantage. Solar-powered motion lights mount anywhere without wiring and operate indefinitely. LED floodlights (800+ lumens) with adjustable sensitivity and range cover driveways, doors, and dark corners. Place them high enough to prevent easy tampering. In a grid-down scenario, solar motion lights continue working when everything else is dark — making your perimeter conspicuously watched while neighbors' properties go black.
Solar: no wiring needed 800+ lumens Price: $15–40 each

Personal defense tools are the last resort — behind awareness, avoidance, and de-escalation. But when those fail, having an effective tool and the training to use it matters. This section focuses on non-lethal and less-lethal options that are legal in most jurisdictions, effective when deployed correctly, and appropriate for a preparedness context. Always check your local laws before purchasing, and always get training with any defense tool you carry.

Pepper Spray / OC Spray
Most Accessible Defense Tool
OC (oleoresin capsicum) spray causes immediate, involuntary eye closure, coughing, and pain — incapacitating an attacker at a distance of 10–15 feet for 20–45 minutes. Legal in all 50 U.S. states (some restrictions on size/concentration apply). Stream pattern is preferred over cone/fog for outdoor use — less blowback, more range. Sabre and POM are the leading brands. Replace annually — propellant degrades over time. Carry it where you can reach it in one second: clipped to a pocket, in a holster, or on a keychain. Buried in the bottom of a bag is useless.
10–15 ft range Replace annually Legal in all 50 states
Tactical Flashlights
Dual-Use: Light + Defense
A high-output flashlight (1,000+ lumens) with a strobe function is a legitimate defensive tool. In darkness, a sudden blast of bright light causes temporary flash blindness and disorientation — buying 3–5 seconds to create distance or assess a threat. The strobe mode amplifies the effect. Beyond defense: it's also the best flashlight you'll own for everyday use. Crenulated bezels (the aggressive crown around the lens) provide a striking surface as a last resort. SureFire, Streamlight, and Fenix make proven tactical lights. Carry it daily — a flashlight you have beats a weapon you left at home.
1,000+ lumens Strobe mode Dual-use: light + defense
Personal Alarms
Attract Attention, Create Distance
Compact devices that emit a 120–140 dB alarm when activated — loud enough to cause pain at close range and audible from several blocks away. The goal isn't to fight — it's to attract attention and startle an attacker long enough to escape. Pin-pull activation means one motion triggers it, even under stress. Ideal for family members who aren't comfortable with other defense tools — children, elderly, or anyone who wants a simple, non-violent option. Attach one to every family member's keychain or bag. She's Birdie and KOSIN are popular options under $15.
120–140 dB Pin-pull activation Price: $8–15
Bear Spray
Maximum Range & Volume
Bear spray is OC spray on a much larger scale — canisters deliver a 20–30 foot fog of concentrated capsaicin designed to stop a charging bear. In a defensive context, it offers significantly more range, volume, and stopping power than standard pepper spray. A single canister provides 7–9 seconds of continuous spray. Legal to carry in most areas, though designed and marketed for animal defense. Counter Assault and UDAP are the trusted brands. Best for property defense and outdoor scenarios where maximum standoff distance matters. Large size means it lives in a home or vehicle rather than a pocket.
20–30 ft range 7–9 sec continuous spray Best use: property, outdoors

Securing your valuables, documents, and defense tools is as important as having them. A safe protects against theft, fire, and unauthorized access — including by children. Proper secure storage also supports operational security (OPSEC): if nobody can see what you have, nobody knows to come take it. Match the safe to what you're storing and the threats you're protecting against.

Home Safes (Fire & Burglary)
Documents, Cash & Valuables
Fire-rated safes protect documents, passports, cash, hard drives, and precious metals from both theft and fire. A UL-rated fire safe withstands 1,200°F+ for 30–60 minutes — enough to survive most house fires. For burglary resistance, look for solid steel construction, pry-resistant doors, and bolt-down hardware. A safe that isn't bolted to the floor is just a heavy box someone can carry out. SentrySafe and Honeywell make affordable home safes; Liberty and Fort Knox are the step-ups. Size it for what you need today plus room for growth.
UL fire-rated Bolt-down essential Price: $50–500
Quick-Access Safes
Seconds Matter
Small safes designed for rapid access — biometric (fingerprint), RFID, or mechanical push-button locks that open in 1–2 seconds. Primarily used for firearms storage near the bed, but also useful for any high-value item you need access to immediately. Biometric locks work without remembering a code; mechanical locks work without batteries. The best models (like Vaultek and Fort Knox) use redundant access methods: fingerprint + code + backup key. Mount them to furniture or wall studs. The goal is fast access for you, zero access for everyone else.
1–2 second access Biometric / code / key Mount to studs or furniture
Lockboxes & Portable Safes
Security on the Move
Smaller lockable containers for vehicles, travel, and temporary storage. Cable-tethered lockboxes secure to vehicle seat frames or fixed objects — preventing smash-and-grab theft of valuables left in a car. Portable safes with TSA-approved locks work for travel. Pelican-style waterproof cases with integrated locks protect documents and electronics from both water and unauthorized access. For vehicle kits: a cable-lock box under the seat stores defense tools, documents, or cash securely when you're away from the vehicle.
Cable-tether to vehicle Best use: cars, travel Price: $25–80
